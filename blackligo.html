Reconstruction of Phenomenological Distribution of Binary Black Holes using Bayesian Neural Network

Project Description

The rapid advancement in gravitational wave detectors’ sensitivity is lead- ing to a significant increase in the detection of binary mergers. With the up- coming O4 observing run, we expect the catalog to expand to approximately 300 events, providing a treasure trove of data. These events are vital for unrav- eling the mysteries of binary black hole populations, and our study’s findings make a substantial contribution to this understanding. By harnessing the power of state-of-the-art Bayesian statistical methods and Neural Networks, we have not only achieved a remarkable reduction in computational costs but also en- sured robust and accurate inferences about the population, thereby pushing the boundaries of our knowledge
